Publications

Assessing Sources of Bias: A Systemic Review of Large Language Model Bias in a Real Estate Context

Assessing Sources of Bias: A Systemic Review of Large Language Model Bias in a Real Estate Context, covers preliminary results from a foundational research effort examining how large language models (LLMs) behave within real estate contexts. The poster focuses on phase 1 of the multi-stage work, examining advertising bias in model generated property descriptions across seven open and closed source LLMs. This work also introduces a generalizable decomposition framework to help identify and isolate social and contextual factors that may contribute to LLM bias when these systems interact with real-world deployment.

Generative AI (GenAI) advances bring both benefits and risks to the housing sector, such as potential discrimination, misleading listings, and restricted access. The National Fair Housing Alliance (NFHA) joined the NIST GenAI Text-to-Text Discriminator Challenge to help address these issues. Our results show that models like SVM and XGBoost can partially distinguish AI-generated from human text, but tracing AI content origins remains difficult, raising concerns about accountability. These findings highlight the need for improved regulation, transparency, detection tools, and awareness in high-risk housing applications. NFHA continues to research and promote responsible GenAI use in housing.
